\section{Job Market Paper}
\textbf{``Beyond Fares: Multimarket Contact and Capacity Discipline in Airline Markets''}\\[3pt]
This paper investigates whether multimarket contact (MMC) facilitates tacit coordination on both price and non-price margins (flight frequency), and whether such conduct varies across firm types in the airline industry. Using a joint structural model of demand, fares, and schedules, instrumenting for MMC using predetermined carrier access to airport gates, I find that legacy carriers' conduct weights increase from near-zero to approximately 0.4 as MMC rises, whereas low-cost carriers consistently price as Nash--Bertrand competitors. Counterfactual simulations indicate that eliminating MMC-induced coordination would increase annualized consumer surplus by roughly 7.8\% and total welfare by 3.5\%.
